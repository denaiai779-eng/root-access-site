How It Works

A Full Day of Learning, Planned for You

Every daily lesson follows this structure. Just open the book and go. Each block is scripted with parent dialogue, student activities, and answer keys included.

9:00 AM 30 min

Reading & Comprehension

Diverse read-alouds with guided questions, vocabulary, and discussion prompts featuring characters from many backgrounds.

9:30 AM 15 min

Spelling & Word Study

Pattern-based spelling with word sorts, dictation sentences, and morphology connections.

9:45 AM 20 min

Grammar & Language

Mentor sentences, parts of speech, punctuation, and sentence structure through real-world examples.

10:05 AM 25 min

Writing Workshop

Narrative, informational, and opinion writing with graphic organizers, drafting guides, and revision checklists.

10:30 AM 15 min

Snack & Movement Break

Step away, stretch, grab a snack. Built in so you never have to wonder when to pause.

10:45 AM 45 min

Math (Singapore Method)

Concrete to pictorial to abstract. Scripted lessons, original problems, answer keys, and challenge extensions.

11:30 AM 40 min

Science (Mon/Wed) or Social Studies (Tue/Thu)

Hands-on experiments with household materials, or de-colonized history with Indigenous perspectives and multiple viewpoints.

Total: approximately 3.5 hours per day. Afternoons are free for play, field trips, co-ops, or enrichment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You Need to Know

Do I need a teaching background?

+

Not at all. Every lesson includes word-for-word scripted dialogue. We tell you exactly what to say, what to ask, and what to do if your child is confused. You read the page at breakfast and you're ready to teach by 9 AM.

What does "de-colonized" actually mean here?

+

It means Indigenous peoples are presented as civilizations with governance, trade networks, and agency — not as footnotes before "real" history starts. The Great Migration, labor movements, and civil rights are taught through the eyes of the people who lived them. We don't do "Columbus discovered America." We tell the whole story, with care and accuracy.

What subjects are included?

+

All seven core subjects: Reading, Math (Singapore Math methodology), Grammar, Science (Next Generation Science Standards with hands-on experiments), Social Studies (de-colonized, Michigan-focused but applicable anywhere), Writing, and Spelling. Everything in one book — no supplementing needed.

Is this aligned to standards?

+

Yes. Aligned to Common Core Math, Next Generation Science Standards, Michigan Social Studies GLCEs, and Common Core ELA. Every lesson tags exactly which standards it addresses. A full coverage map is included with the Starter Pack. While Michigan-specific in social studies, the math, science, and ELA standards are applicable nationwide.

How long does each day take?

+

About 3.5 hours of structured learning, including a built-in break. That leaves your entire afternoon free for co-ops, field trips, music lessons, sports, or just being a kid. The schedule is flexible — you can shift blocks around to fit your family's rhythm.

What if my child is ahead or behind grade level?

+

Every lesson includes built-in differentiation: extension activities and harder problems for kids who are ahead, and scaffolding with concrete re-entry points for those who need more support. We help you diagnose exactly where the gap is so you can address it without starting over.

Is this a religious curriculum?

+

No. Root Access is a secular curriculum. We respect all belief systems and don't promote any religious perspective. Our focus is on accurate, inclusive, standards-aligned academic content that welcomes every family.

Will more weeks be available?

+

Yes. New weeks release regularly, building toward a full 36-week school year. When you download the free week, you'll be first to know when new content launches.
